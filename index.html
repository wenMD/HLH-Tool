Risk-Stratified Treatment Algorithm

üü¢ Mild-Moderate HLH/MAS

Clinical Features: Stable vitals, not critically ill, low infection risk

First-Line Options (choose 1-2):
  • IVIG: 1-2 g/kg (especially if infection concern)
  • Anakinra: 1-2 mg/kg q12h (max 100mg q12h)
  • Methylprednisolone: 1-2 mg/kg/day OR
  • Dexamethasone: 5-10 mg/m¬≤/day (HLH-2004 protocol dosing)
  • Cyclosporine: 2-5 mg/kg q12h
Monitor closely: Response expected within 48-72 hours. Consider escalation if no improvement.

üü° Severe HLH/MAS

Clinical Features: ICU-level care, multi-organ dysfunction, hemodynamic instability

Combination Therapy:
  • Methylprednisolone: 2-10 mg/kg/day OR
  • Dexamethasone: 5-10 mg/m¬≤/day (HLH-2004 protocol)
  • PLUS Anakinra: 2-4 mg/kg q12h
  • Consider adding IVIG: 1-2 g/kg
  • Consider Cyclosporine: 2-5 mg/kg q12h
Escalation if no response in 24-48 hours: Consider etoposide, tocilizumab, or other agents

Required Screening & Prophylaxis:
  • Pre-therapy: CBC, BUN, Cr, AST, ALT, ferritin, fibrinogen, D-dimer
  • Monitor CXCL9: Prior to therapy, then weekly
  • TB screening: Interferon-gamma release assay
  • Viral monitoring: Adenovirus, EBV, CMV PCRs every 2 weeks
Required Prophylaxis:
  • Zoster: Valacyclovir 15-20 mg/kg/dose PO BID (max 500 mg/dose)
  • PJP: Bactrim 5 mg/kg/dose PO MWF (max 160 mg/dose)
  • Fungal: Fluconazole 3-6 mg/kg/dose PO once daily (max 400 mg/dose)
Concurrent Medications (Boston Children's Protocol):
  • Dexamethasone: 5-10 mg/m¬≤/day (mandatory background therapy)
  • Cyclosporine: Often used concurrently
  • Avoid: JAK inhibitors and other biologics (lack of safety evidence with emapalumab)
Alternative Salvage Options:
  • Etoposide: 150 mg/m¬≤ 2x/week (HLH-2004 protocol)
  • Tocilizumab: 8-12 mg/kg (max 800mg) every 2 weeks
  • Ruxolitinib: 5-10 mg BID (avoid with emapalumab)
  • Alemtuzumab: 1 mg/kg divided over 4 days
Emapalumab Monitoring & Precautions:
  • Monitor for infections (TB, HSV, Pneumocystis, fungi) every 2 weeks
  • Most common AEs: Infections (56%), hypertension (41%), infusion reactions (27%)1
  • Contraindicated in active TB, disseminated histoplasmosis
  • Ongoing clinical monitoring for herpes zoster, PJP, and fungal infections

‚ö†Ô∏è Definition of Refractory HLH/MAS

Refractory Disease Definition:

  • Failed ‚â•2 treatments in Table 1 (Boston Children's criteria)
  • CXCL9 elevated (biomarker of treatment failure)
  • Inadequate response to first-line therapy after 72 hours
  • Progressive disease despite appropriate treatment
  • Recurrent disease after initial response
  • Intolerance to conventional HLH therapy

Clinical Indicators of Treatment Failure:

  • Persistent or recurring fever
  • Worsening or static cytopenias
  • Rising or persistently elevated ferritin
  • No improvement in inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR)
  • Progressive organ dysfunction
  • Need for escalating supportive care
Action: If criteria for refractory disease are met, immediately consider salvage therapies including emapalumab per Boston Children's Table 2 protocol.
Important Considerations:
  • Malignancy-associated HLH has poor prognosis (100% mortality in some series)
  • Consider oncologic evaluation in new-onset HLH/MAS (~12% have malignancy)
  • Genetic testing recommended in most patients (25% yield in Boston Children's series)
  • IVIG particularly useful when infection concern limits immunosuppression
